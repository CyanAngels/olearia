Obtaining DAISY books

If you are blind, have low vision or a print disability such as dyslexia DAISY digital talking books can be obtained from your national or regional library for the blind. Below is a short and incomplete of these


  • Australia

    • Vision Australia (serve patrons in Victoria, New South Wales, Queensland, ACT and the Northern Territories) http://www.visionaustralia/http://www.visionaustralia.org
    • Association for the Blind of Western Australia http://www.abwa.asn.au/
    • The Royal Society for the Blind (RSB) South Australia http://www.rsb.org.au/http://www.rsb.org.au/
  • Canada

    • Canadian National Institute for the Blind (CNIB) http://www.cnib.ca/http://www.cnib.ca/
  • United Kingdom

    • National Institute for the Blind (CNIB)
      hrefhttp://www.rnib.org.uk/http://www.rnib.org.uk
  • United States (at this time books from neitehr the NLS or RFB&D are playable on Olearia. We are work to support RFB&D books. The NLS has indicated that it will not support software based playback on computers.

    • National Library Service (NLS)
      hrefhttp://www.loc.gov/nls/http://www.loc.gov/nls/
    • Recordings for the Blind and Dyslexic (RFB&D) http://www.rfbd.org/http://www.rfbd.org
  • Sweden

    • Talboks- och punktskriftsbiblioteket (TPB) http://www.tpb.se/http://www.tpb.se
  • Denmark

    • Dansk Elektronisk Blindebibliotek (DEBBI) http://www.debbi.dk/http://www.debbi.dk/
  • South Africa

    • South African Library for the Blind http://www.blindlib.org.za/http://www.blindlib.org.za/
  • China

    • China Digital Library for Visual Impairment
      hrefhttp://www.cdlvi.cn/http://www.cdlvi.cn/


In addition DAISY books for both the disabled as well as the general public can be downloaded from the Beyond Books Beyond Barriers project of the Association for the Blind of Western Australia and Association for the Blind of Western Australia State Library of Western Australia at http://www.cucat.org/library/http://www.cucat.org/library/
